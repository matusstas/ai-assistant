# COMPONENT ACCESS (CONT.)

## Selector Switches
1. Complete the steps 1-9 from Panels removal instructions.
2. Remove the Power Cord.
3. Remove the Water Inlet Valve and Thermistor.
4. Remove the Motor Relay.
5. Remove the knob from the front of the control panel. There is a flat spot on the shaft and the knob for proper orientation.
6. Lift the control panel and lay it forward.
7. Disconnect the wire harness connection. Grasp all the wires as close to the connection as possible and use a finger on the other hand to help pull the connector out. The connector is keyed for proper orientation during reinstallation.
8. Lift the locking tab and rotate the temperature selector switch 1/8 of a turn.
9. Pull switch out of hole in the control panel.

## Central Control Unit Connections
1. Complete the steps 1-10 from Panels removal instructions.
2. Remove the Power Cord.
3. Remove the Water Inlet Valve and Thermistor.
4. Remove the Motor Relay and Temperature Selector Switch.
5. Remove J7 power plug by pulling it off of the connector. There is no lock on this plug.
   
   A. J7 power plug  
   B. J16 connector

6. Press the two (2) locking tabs and disconnect plug at the J16 connector. This connector supplies power and control to components in the cabinet of the washing machine.
7. Press the two (2) locking tabs and pull the lid lock plug off the connector at J15.
   
   A. J3 Connector  
   B. J15 Connector

8. Press the locking tab and pull the plug for both the thermistor and water valves off the connector at J3. The thermistor is part of this wire harness. The replacement part includes the wire harness for the hot and cold water inlet valves and the thermistor.
